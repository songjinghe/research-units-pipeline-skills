# Opener Catalog

Use these as modes, not sentence templates.

## Tension-first

Use when:
- the subsection centers on a trade-off
- the reader needs to know why labels alone are misleading

Job:
- state the tension and end paragraph 1 with the thesis

## Contrast-first

Use when:
- the field clearly splits into two routes
- the comparison itself is the main organizing move

Job:
- open with the split, then explain why it matters

## Protocol-first

Use when:
- evaluation settings determine whether claims are comparable
- tool access, budgets, or threat models change interpretation

Job:
- foreground the condition that makes comparison meaningful

## Failure-first

Use when:
- a recurring failure mode motivates the lens better than a neutral topic sentence

Job:
- start with the failure surface, then connect it to design choices

## Decision-first

Use when:
- the subsection is most useful as guidance about when to choose one route over another

Job:
- frame the practical decision and then justify it with evidence

## Avoid

Avoid opener defaults such as:
- `This subsection ...`
- `In this subsection, we ...`
- `This section provides an overview ...`
- repeated `Taken together, ...` style synthesis stems in paragraph 1
