# Limitation Moves

## Purpose

Limitations should change how the reader interprets the subsection, not act as boilerplate cleanup.

## Reliable limitation moves

### Protocol boundary
- explain which missing or shifting protocol detail weakens comparison

### Transfer boundary
- explain when the result may not carry across settings, tools, or environments

### Evidence boundary
- explain what remains unclear because evidence is sparse, abstract-level, or weakly reported

### Failure boundary
- explain which failure mode remains exposed even when the route looks strong

## Placement rule

A limitation can live:
- as the last sentence of a contrast paragraph
- as a dedicated caveat paragraph
- as part of the local conclusion

Do not force a dedicated limitation paragraph if the caveat fits better inside the comparison.

## Avoid

Avoid slot phrasing such as:
- `Two limitations ...`
- `Three takeaways ...`
- generic `future work should ...` closers
