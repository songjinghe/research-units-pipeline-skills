# Contrast Moves

## Goal

Make comparisons explicit and evidence-bearing.
Do not summarize route A and route B in isolation when a direct contrast is possible.

## Reliable contrast moves

### Mechanism contrast
- compare what each route changes in the system
- then connect that change to an observable consequence

### Assumption contrast
- compare what must be true for each route to work
- highlight interface, data, tool, or budget assumptions

### Evaluation contrast
- compare what each route looks strong or weak on
- keep task, metric, and constraint near the claim

### Failure contrast
- compare where each route breaks, not just where it succeeds

## Good comparison discipline

Prefer:
- one axis per contrast-heavy paragraph
- direct connective language (`whereas`, `in contrast`, `unlike`, `while`)
- citations attached to the sentence that makes the contrast
- reader-facing axis language normalized into natural prose (`and`, `across`, `under`) instead of brief-style slash labels

Avoid:
- slash-list axis labels copied from briefs
- `many works do X, while others do Y` with no concrete consequence
- trailing cite dumps with no comparison claim
