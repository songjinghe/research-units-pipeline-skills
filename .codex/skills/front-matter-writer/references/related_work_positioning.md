# Related Work Positioning

## Core function

Related Work should position the survey through its comparison lens, not through a shopping list of adjacent papers.

## Preferred structure

### Group by lens, not by publication type

Good grouping units:
- interface and execution assumptions
- planning, memory, or adaptation as comparison-bearing mechanisms
- evaluation, reproducibility, or risk as calibration layers

Less useful default grouping:
- “prior surveys” as a standalone bucket
- chronological recaps without a comparison reason

## Positioning jobs

A good Related Work section should:
- show what nearby surveys or benchmark papers already clarify
- show where they stop short of the current lens
- connect neighboring strands without flattening them into synonyms
- end with a gap statement about comparison meaning, not just topic coverage

## Gap statement rule

End with a gap that matters for interpretation, such as:
- mismatched protocols block clean comparison
- shared labels hide different interface assumptions
- evaluation settings make gains hard to compare or transfer

Avoid gap statements like:
- `the literature is still growing`
- `more work is needed`
- `few studies have considered everything`

## Prior-survey handling

Do not create a “Prior Surveys” mini-bucket by default.
Instead:
- cite prior surveys inside the lens paragraph they inform
- treat them as evidence for what the field already foregrounds or misses
- keep the current survey’s wedge explicit

## Writer reminder

The best Related Work paragraphs position the reader, not the author.
If a paragraph mostly says what “this survey” is doing, it probably belongs in the Introduction instead.
