# Good Examples

Paraphrase these patterns; do not copy them verbatim.

## Abstract opener

Good:
- `Recent systems are often compared under labels that hide different interface and evaluation assumptions, making headline gains harder to interpret across settings.`

Why it works:
- starts with the comparison problem
- makes the lens immediately useful
- avoids self-narration

## Introduction methodology note

Good:
- `We retrieved studies from a bounded recent window, narrowed them to a curated core set for close reading, and treat protocol detail as part of the substantive comparison rather than as appendix metadata.`

Why it works:
- reads like survey methodology, not a run log
- keeps the note compact
- ties method to interpretation

## Related Work positioning sentence

Good:
- `Existing surveys map the field’s main system families, but they often stop before making tool assumptions, budget limits, and evaluation settings comparable on the same scale.`

Why it works:
- positions prior work fairly
- states the wedge in reader-facing terms
- avoids a list of survey names

## Discussion synthesis sentence

Good:
- `Across the literature, the most stable pattern is not a single winning design but a recurring dependence on how interface choices and evaluation conditions are coupled.`

Why it works:
- cross-cutting claim
- comparative rather than chapter-by-chapter recap
- specific enough to matter
