# Bad Examples

These are anti-patterns to rewrite, not templates to preserve.

## Bad abstract opener

Bad:
- `This survey provides an overview of recent work in the area and is organized into several sections.`

Why it fails:
- pure self-narration
- no lens, no boundary, no claim
- sounds interchangeable with any survey

## Bad introduction method note

Bad:
- `This run uses abstract-only evidence and follows the pipeline contract for later stages.`

Why it fails:
- leaks internal process terms
- reads like tooling metadata
- does not help the reader interpret claims

## Bad related work positioning

Bad:
- `Prior surveys discuss many related topics, and we also review those surveys here.`

Why it fails:
- redundant and circular
- no positioning wedge
- no reason for the grouping

## Bad discussion closer

Bad:
- `Future work should continue exploring this area in more depth.`

Why it fails:
- generic and non-comparative
- gives the reader no concrete interpretive takeaway
- could close almost any paper
