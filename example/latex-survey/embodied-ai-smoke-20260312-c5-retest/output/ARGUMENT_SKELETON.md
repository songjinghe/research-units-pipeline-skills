# Argument skeleton

## Consistency Contract

- Use one stable naming scheme for comparable agent components, evaluation settings, and benchmark references.
- Keep evaluation claims tied to task, metric, and protocol constraints rather than architecture labels alone.
- Treat limitations as part of the argument, not as detachable boilerplate.
- Keep subsection comparisons chapter-scoped unless a citation is globally in-scope.

## Chapter dependencies

- Problem Settings & Embodiment: Manipulation, navigation, and task scope; Observation, action, and embodiment interfaces; Task diversity, environment realism, and transfer constraints
- Model & Policy Architectures: Vision-language-action and policy backbones; World models, planning, and reasoning; Transfer across embodiments and viewpoints
- Data, Training & Post-Training: Pretraining data and supervision; Post-training, feedback, and continual improvement; Data quality, scaling, and distribution shift
- Evaluation, Safety & Deployment: Benchmarks, metrics, and generalization; Safety, reliability, and real-world deployment; Failure analysis, robustness, and stress testing
